CANADA UPHOLDS COUNTERVAIL DUTY ON US CORN OTTAWA MARCH 6 THE CANADIAN IMPORT TRIBUNAL RULED TODAY SUBSIDIZED US CORN IMPORTS WERE INJURIOUS TO CANADIAN GROWERS AND UPHELD A COUNTERVAILING DUTY OF 849 US CTS A BUSHEL SET EARLIER THIS YEAR THE RULING IS THE RESULT OF TRADE ACTION LAUNCHED IN 1985 BY THE ONTARIO CORN PRODUCERS ASSOCIATION THAT CONTENDED US SUBSIDIES WERE DRIVING AMERICAN CORN PRICES BELOW CANADIAN PRODUCTION COSTS A SPOKESMAN FOR THE DEPARTMENT OF REVENUE SAID A STUDY OF THE LEVEL OF THE DUTY WILL BE UNDERTAKEN IN THE NEXT SIX MONTHS TO DETERMINE IF IT SHOULD BE ADJUSTED
