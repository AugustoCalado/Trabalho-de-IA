HARLEY DAVIDSON  HDI SEEKS IMPORT RELIEF END WASHINGTON MARCH 17 HARLEY DAVIDSON INC SAID IT IS ASKING THE INTERNATIONAL TRADE COMMISSION TO TERMINATE IMPORT RELIEF FOR HEAVYWEIGHT MOTORCYCLES THE COMPANY SAID IT HAS REGAINED THE LEADERSHIP IN SALES OF THE PREMIUM SEGMENT OF THE HEAVYWEIGHT MOTORCYCLE MARKET AND NO LONGER NEEDS SPECIAL TARIFFS TO COMPETE WITH OVERSEAS COMPETITORS  HARLEY IS THE ONLY US MOTORCYCLE MAKER THE TARIFFS WERE IMPOSED IN 1983 WHEN THE ITC FOUND THAT JAPANESE COMPETITORS HAD BEEN FLOODING THE MARKET
