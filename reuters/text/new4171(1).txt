TAIWAN TO RELAX IMPORT CURBS ON MORE GOODS TAIPEI MARCH 12 TAIWAN WILL RELAX IMPORT CONTROLS ON MORE FOREIGN GOODS A GOVERNMENT SPOKESMAN SAID TODAY THE MOVE WAS TO ALLOW GREATER ACCESS TO TAIWAN MARKETS BY OVERSEAS TRADING PARTNERS ESPECIALLY THE UNITED STATES AN OFFICIAL OF THE COUNCIL FOR ECONOMIC PLANNING AND DEVELOPMENT TOLD S IMPORT CURBS ON ABOUT 400 FOREIGN GOODS INCLUDING STATIONERY AND BOOKS WOULD SOON BE RELAXED HE SAID TAIWAN ANNOUNCED IT WOULD EASE CURBS ON SOME 600 FARMING AND INDUSTRIAL PRODUCTS LAST MONTH THE OFFICIAL SAID THE MOVES WERE INTENDED TO BALANCE TRADE BETWEEN TAIWAN AND THE UNITED STATES AND OTHER TRADING PARTNERS TAIWAN'S TRADE SURPLUS REACHED A RECORD 156 BILLION DLRS LAST YEAR UP FROM 1062 BILLION IN 1985
